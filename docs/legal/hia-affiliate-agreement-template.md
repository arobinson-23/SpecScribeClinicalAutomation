## RECITALS

**WHEREAS:**

A. The Custodian is a health information custodian as defined in HIA s. 1(1)(f) and collects, uses, and discloses individually identifiable health information ("Health Information") in the course of providing health services to its patients.

B. The Affiliate provides an AI-powered clinical documentation, coding, and compliance SaaS platform operating under the trade name **SpecScribe** (the "**Platform**"), and the Custodian wishes to engage the Affiliate to process Health Information on its behalf through the Platform.

C. The Affiliate and its employees, contractors, and agents who access Health Information in the course of providing the Platform services are **affiliates** of the Custodian within the meaning of HIA s. 1(1)(a)(iv).

D. Under HIA s. 37, an affiliate of a custodian must: (i) comply with the HIA; (ii) comply with the custodian's directions; (iii) not collect, use, or disclose Health Information except as authorized by the custodian and the HIA; and (iv) implement reasonable safeguards against unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

E. The Parties wish to set out in writing the terms governing the Affiliate's access to, and processing of, Health Information, in compliance with HIA s. 37 and the broader requirements of the HIA.

**NOW THEREFORE**, in consideration of the mutual covenants set out herein and other good and valuable consideration, the receipt and sufficiency of which are acknowledged, the Parties agree as follows:

## PART 2 — AFFILIATE OBLIGATIONS UNDER HIA s. 37

**2.1 Compliance with HIA.** The Affiliate shall, and shall cause each Affiliate Personnel to:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) comply with the HIA and all applicable regulations in all activities involving Health Information;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) comply with the reasonable written directions of the Custodian regarding the collection, use, disclosure, protection, and retention of Health Information, provided such directions are consistent with the HIA; and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) not do anything that would cause the Custodian to be in contravention of the HIA.

**2.2 Acknowledgment of Affiliate Status.** The Affiliate acknowledges that it and each Affiliate Personnel who accesses Health Information in the course of providing the Services is an "affiliate" of the Custodian within the meaning of HIA s. 1(1)(a)(iv), and is subject to the duties imposed on affiliates by HIA s. 37.

**2.3 Custodian's Directions.** The Custodian may, from time to time, issue written directions to the Affiliate regarding the handling of Health Information. The Affiliate shall implement such directions within a commercially reasonable timeframe, and in any event within ten (10) Business Days, unless the direction is required urgently to prevent or mitigate a Breach (in which case promptly). If the Affiliate believes a direction would violate the HIA or any Applicable Privacy Law, it shall notify the Custodian in writing within three (3) Business Days and the Parties shall cooperate in good faith to resolve the conflict.

**2.4 Minimum Necessary.** The Affiliate shall access, collect, use, and process only the minimum amount of Health Information necessary for the Authorized Purpose and no more.

## PART 3 — PURPOSE LIMITATION

**3.1 Authorized Purpose.** The Affiliate is authorized to access and process Health Information solely for the following purposes (collectively, the "**Authorized Purpose**"):

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) providing the Services to the Custodian as described in the MSA, including:
- AI-assisted transcription of clinical encounters;
- generation of draft clinical documentation and notes;
- AI-assisted coding suggestions (CPT/ICD-10-CA);
- prior authorization support; and
- compliance monitoring and reporting for the Custodian's practice;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) troubleshooting, maintenance, and support of the Platform for the Custodian's benefit, to the minimum extent necessary;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) ensuring the security, integrity, and availability of the Platform and the Health Information stored within it; and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(d) complying with applicable legal obligations.

**3.2 Prohibition on Secondary Use.** The Affiliate shall not:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) use Health Information for any purpose other than the Authorized Purpose, including for product development, model training, benchmarking, marketing, analytics, or research, without the prior written consent of the Custodian and, where required, the affected individual;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) sell, license, rent, transfer, or otherwise make available Health Information to any third party, except as expressly permitted under this Agreement with respect to approved Sub-Affiliates (Schedule A) or as required by law;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) combine Health Information with information from other custodians in a manner that could identify individuals; or

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(d) use Health Information to train, fine-tune, or improve any artificial intelligence or machine learning model — including any third-party foundation model — without the prior written consent of the Custodian.

> **[COUNSEL REVIEW FLAG — s. 3.2(d)]**: AI model training on patient data requires free, informed, and specific consent under the HIA and may be subject to additional requirements under PIPEDA and the proposed *Artificial Intelligence and Data Act*. Confirm with counsel whether this prohibition should be absolute or subject to de-identification under HIA s. 62 and De-identification Standards.

**3.3 De-Identified Data.** Nothing in this Agreement prohibits the Affiliate from using health information that has been de-identified in accordance with HIA s. 62 and the *Health Information Regulation*, AR 70/2001, s. 5, for product improvement, analytics, or research, provided the information is de-identified before any such use and cannot reasonably be re-identified.

## PART 4 — SECURITY SAFEGUARDS

**4.1 Required Safeguards.** The Affiliate shall maintain the Security Safeguards set out in Schedule C at all times during this Agreement. As a minimum, the Affiliate shall:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) **Encryption at rest**: Encrypt all Health Information stored within the Platform using AES-256-GCM encryption at the application layer prior to writing to any database or storage system;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) **Encryption in transit**: Transmit all Health Information exclusively over encrypted channels using TLS 1.3 or higher. No Health Information shall be transmitted over unencrypted connections;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) **Multi-factor authentication ("MFA")**: Require mandatory MFA (TOTP or equivalent) for all Affiliate Personnel accounts that can access Health Information. No single-factor authentication bypass shall be permitted;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(d) **Session management**: Enforce a 15-minute idle session timeout and a 24-hour absolute session limit for all user sessions that access Health Information;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(e) **Audit logging**: Maintain comprehensive audit logs of all access to, and operations performed on, Health Information, including the user identity, timestamp, IP address, action type, and resource identifier. Audit logs shall not contain Health Information values (only field names and identifiers);

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(f) **Access controls**: Implement role-based access controls (RBAC) limiting each Affiliate Personnel's access to the minimum Health Information necessary for their role. Provider-level users shall access only their own patients' records;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(g) **Data residency**: Store and process all Health Information exclusively within Canada (AWS ca-central-1, Montreal, Quebec), with no cross-border transfer of Health Information, except as expressly permitted with respect to Sub-Affiliates in Schedule A and only with adequate contractual and technical safeguards;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(h) **Vulnerability management**: Apply critical security patches within fifteen (15) calendar days of availability; apply high-severity patches within thirty (30) days;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(i) **Backup and recovery**: Maintain regular automated backups of Health Information with the capability to restore full system functionality within seventy-two (72) hours of a disruptive event; and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(j) **Personnel training**: Ensure all Affiliate Personnel with access to Health Information receive privacy and security training appropriate to their role upon onboarding and annually thereafter.

**4.2 Safeguard Reviews.** The Affiliate shall review its Security Safeguards at least annually and following any material security incident or significant change to the Platform, and shall update safeguards as appropriate to address identified risks.

**4.3 No PHI in Logs.** The Affiliate shall implement technical controls to prevent Health Information values (including patient names, dates of birth, personal health numbers, diagnoses, and clinical content) from appearing in application logs, error messages, or monitoring systems. Only field names, encounter identifiers, and practice identifiers may appear in logs.

**4.4 AI Minimization.** Where Health Information is processed through AI or machine learning components of the Platform, the Affiliate shall implement data minimization controls to strip or pseudonymize direct identifiers (name, date of birth, personal health number, address) before transmitting clinical content to any AI inference endpoint, to the extent technically feasible while still enabling the Authorized Purpose.

## PART 9 — BREACH NOTIFICATION

**9.1 Affiliate's Obligation to Notify.** The Affiliate shall notify the Custodian of any Breach affecting Health Information in the Affiliate's custody or control as follows:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) **Within 24 hours** of the Affiliate becoming aware of a confirmed or reasonably suspected Breach involving: unauthorized access to Health Information; exfiltration, publication, or sale of Health Information; or any system compromise affecting the integrity or availability of Health Information.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) **Initial notice** may be verbal, immediately followed by written notice to the Custodian Contact within the same 24-hour window.

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) **Supplemental notice** shall be provided as additional information becomes available.

> **[COUNSEL REVIEW FLAG — s. 9.1]**: HIA s. 60 requires the Custodian to notify the Office of the Information and Privacy Commissioner of Alberta ("OIPC") and affected individuals if the Custodian determines there is a "real risk of significant harm." The 24-hour Affiliate-to-Custodian notification period is designed to give the Custodian adequate time to conduct its own assessment and meet the HIA s. 60 notification obligations. Counsel should confirm whether this timeline is sufficient in light of any applicable OIPC guidance on breach notification timelines.

**9.2 Content of Breach Notice.** The Affiliate's written Breach notice shall include, to the extent then known:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) date and time of discovery of the Breach;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) a description of the nature of the Breach (what happened);

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) the categories and estimated number of individuals whose Health Information may have been affected;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(d) the categories of Health Information potentially involved;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(e) the likely cause of the Breach;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(f) steps taken or proposed to contain, remediate, and prevent recurrence of the Breach;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(g) contact information for the Affiliate's privacy officer or incident response lead; and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(h) any known or estimated impact on the Custodian's operations or the affected individuals.

**9.3 Cooperation.** Following a Breach, the Affiliate shall:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) cooperate fully with the Custodian's investigation and any investigation by the OIPC or other regulatory authority;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) take all commercially reasonable steps to contain and remediate the Breach at the Affiliate's expense;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) preserve all evidence related to the Breach; and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(d) provide a written Breach investigation report to the Custodian within fifteen (15) Business Days of the Breach's discovery (or such longer period as the Custodian may agree in writing), setting out the Affiliate's root cause analysis and remediation measures.

**9.4 Custodian's Notification Obligations.** For clarity, the Custodian (not the Affiliate) is responsible for assessing whether the Breach creates a "real risk of significant harm" under HIA s. 60 and for making any required notification to the OIPC and affected individuals. The Affiliate shall provide all reasonable assistance requested by the Custodian for this purpose.

## PART 10 — INDEMNIFICATION

**10.1 Affiliate Indemnity.** The Affiliate shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Custodian and its officers, directors, employees, and agents from and against any third-party claims, losses, damages, fines, penalties, and reasonable legal costs arising out of or related to:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) a breach by the Affiliate or any Affiliate Personnel of this Agreement or Applicable Privacy Law;

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) a Breach caused or contributed to by the negligence or wilful misconduct of the Affiliate or any Sub-Affiliate; or

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(c) any unauthorized collection, use, or disclosure of Health Information by the Affiliate or any Sub-Affiliate.

**10.2 Custodian Indemnity.** The Custodian shall indemnify, defend, and hold harmless the Affiliate from and against any third-party claims arising out of or related to:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) the Custodian's own breach of the HIA or Applicable Privacy Law independent of the Affiliate's acts or omissions; or

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) Health Information provided by the Custodian that was collected or held in violation of the HIA.

> **[COUNSEL REVIEW FLAG — Part 10]**: HIA s. 37 creates independent statutory duties on both custodians and affiliates. The HIA does not create a clear statutory indemnification framework — this indemnification regime is contractual and its enforceability, particularly in relation to OIPC orders and administrative penalties, should be reviewed by Alberta privacy counsel. Note that under HIA s. 66, the Information and Privacy Commissioner may investigate and make binding orders, and fines under HIA s. 74 can reach $50,000 for individuals and $500,000 for organizations. Confirm that the indemnification language does not inadvertently limit the Custodian's ability to comply with regulatory orders.

**10.3 Limitation of Liability.** [Insert cross-reference to limitation of liability provisions in the MSA or negotiate separately.] The indemnification obligations in this Part 10 are subject to the limitation of liability provisions in the MSA, except that no limitation of liability shall apply to:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) a Breach caused by the Affiliate's wilful misconduct or gross negligence; or

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) the Affiliate's unauthorized sale or commercial exploitation of Health Information.

## PART 13 — DATA RETENTION AND DESTRUCTION

**13.1 Retention Period.** The Affiliate shall retain Health Information processed through the Platform for the period directed by the Custodian, which shall be not less than ten (10) years from the date the Health Information was created or last amended (the "**Retention Period**"), consistent with the requirements of applicable Alberta health regulation for medical records.

**13.2 No Retention Beyond Authorized Purpose.** The Affiliate shall not retain Health Information in identifiable form beyond the Retention Period or, if earlier, the termination of this Agreement and completion of the obligations in s. 13.4.

**13.3 Return of Health Information.** Upon written request by the Custodian at any time, or upon termination or expiry of this Agreement, the Affiliate shall, within thirty (30) Business Days:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) provide the Custodian with an export of all Health Information in the Platform in a machine-readable, portable format (e.g., HL7 FHIR R4 or structured JSON); and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) confirm in writing the completeness of the export.

**13.4 Secure Destruction.** Following expiry of the Retention Period or, if earlier, within ninety (90) days after completion of the data export under s. 13.3, the Affiliate shall:

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(a) securely destroy or irreversibly de-identify all Health Information in its custody and control (including all backup copies and copies held by Sub-Affiliates), using a method that renders the information irrecoverable (e.g., cryptographic erasure of encryption keys, NIST 800-88 media sanitization); and

&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;(b) provide the Custodian with a Data Destruction Certificate within fifteen (15) Business Days of completion of destruction, signed by the Affiliate's authorized representative, confirming the scope, method, and date of destruction and identifying each storage system and Sub-Affiliate from which Health Information was destroyed.

**13.5 Legal Hold.** Notwithstanding s. 13.4, the Affiliate may retain Health Information beyond the Retention Period if required by applicable law (including as a result of a regulatory hold or litigation preservation obligation), provided the Affiliate notifies the Custodian in writing of the basis for continued retention and continues to apply all Security Safeguards.

## SCHEDULE C — SECURITY SAFEGUARDS SUMMARY

*This Schedule summarizes the Security Safeguards maintained by the Affiliate as of the Effective Date. The Affiliate will update this Schedule annually or upon any material change.*

| Control | Specification |
|---|---|
| **Encryption at rest** | AES-256-GCM, application-layer encryption before database write. Encryption keys managed via AWS KMS (ca-central-1). Key rotation: 90-day cycle. |
| **Encryption in transit** | TLS 1.3 minimum for all API and browser connections. HTTPS enforced; HSTS header with 2-year max-age. |
| **Authentication** | Clerk (clerk.com) for primary auth. Custom TOTP-based MFA layer mandatory for all users. No account may access Health Information without MFA enrolled. |
| **Session management** | 15-minute idle timeout; 24-hour absolute session limit. Sessions invalidated on logout. Secure, HttpOnly, SameSite=Strict cookies. |
| **Audit logging** | All Health Information access, creation, modification, and deletion events logged with: user ID, practice ID, encounter ID, action type, timestamp, IP address. No PHI values in logs. Logs stored in separate, append-only storage with 7-year retention. |
| **Access control** | RBAC with roles: Provider, Admin, Biller, Staff, SuperAdmin. Providers access only their own patients' records. Admin access requires explicit grant. |
| **Data residency** | All Health Information stored and processed in AWS ca-central-1 (Montreal). No replication to regions outside Canada. |
| **Vulnerability management** | Critical patches: ≤15 days. High-severity patches: ≤30 days. Dependency scanning: automated (Dependabot). Container scanning: Trivy. |
| **Penetration testing** | Annual third-party penetration test. Results available to Custodian under NDA upon request. |
| **Backup and recovery** | Automated daily backups with point-in-time recovery. Cross-AZ replication. RTO target: 72 hours. RPO target: 24 hours. |
| **Sub-Affiliate security** | Written DPA with each Sub-Affiliate. Sub-Affiliates subject to equivalent security standards. |
| **PHI minimization (AI)** | Direct identifiers stripped from clinical content before transmission to AI inference endpoints. Only de-identified or pseudonymized clinical narratives transmitted to Anthropic/Bedrock. |
| **Personnel** | Background checks for all Affiliate Personnel with access to Health Information. Annual privacy training. Confidentiality agreements signed prior to Health Information access. |
| **Incident response** | Written Incident Response Plan maintained and tested annually. 24-hour breach notification to Custodian (s. 9.1). |
